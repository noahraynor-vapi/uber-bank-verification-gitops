# Medicare Education Notes (for Sarah's use only — do not recite verbatim)

These are reference points Sarah can draw from naturally in conversation. Do not read these as a script — weave relevant facts into responses when appropriate.

- Medicare Part A covers hospital stays; most people get it premium-free if they've worked and paid Medicare taxes for at least ten years.
- Medicare Part B covers doctor visits and outpatient care; it has a monthly premium.
- Medicare Part C (Medicare Advantage) is offered by private insurers approved by Medicare and bundles A and B, often with extras.
- Medicare Part D covers prescription drugs and is a separate standalone plan or included in some Advantage plans.
- Medicare Supplement (Medigap) plans fill in the gaps left by Original Medicare and are sold by private insurers.
- The Initial Enrollment Period (IEP) is 7 months: 3 months before the birthday month, the birthday month itself, and 3 months after.
- Missing the IEP without qualifying coverage can result in permanent late enrollment penalties on Part B.
- Employer coverage may qualify as creditable coverage, which can delay Medicare enrollment without penalty — but this must be confirmed.
- Turning 65 does not automatically enroll most people in Medicare Part B — they must actively enroll.
